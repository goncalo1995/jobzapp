**Cover Letter Tips**

</aside>

1. **Tailor Each Application**: Research the company and adjust the cover letter
   to reflect its values and mission.
2. **Quantify Achievements**: Include measurable results to demonstrate your
   impact.
3. **Use Keywords**: Incorporate terms from the job description to pass ATS
   (applicant tracking systems).
4. **Keep it Concise**: Limit to one page, focusing on the most relevant
   experience and skills.

**Key Rules**

</aside>

### **Formatting and Layout**

- **Font Style**: Use clean, professional fonts like Times New Roman, Arial, or
  Calibri.
- **Font Size**: Stick to 10–12 points for readability.
- **Margins**: Use 1-inch margins on all sides.
- **Line Spacing**: Single or 1.15 spacing is ideal, with a blank line between
  paragraphs.

### **Length**

- Keep it to **one page** (3–4 short paragraphs).

### **Tone and Language**

- Use a **formal and professional tone**.
- Avoid jargon, slang, or overly casual expressions.
- Be **clear and concise**—get straight to the point without unnecessary fluff.

### **General Rules**

- **Tailor Each Letter**: Customize it for the job and company.
- **Proofread**: Ensure it’s error-free in grammar, spelling, and punctuation.
- **Consistency**: Match the design (fonts and formatting) of your cover letter
  to your resume.
- **Action-Oriented**: Use strong action verbs to describe your skills and
  achievements.
- **Professional File Name**: Save it as “[YourName]_CoverLetter.pdf.”
